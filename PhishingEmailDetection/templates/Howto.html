📖 How to Use PhishGuard

Your complete guide to exporting emails and analyzing them for threats.

1 Download an Email as an .eml File

PhishGuard requires the raw data of an email to properly analyze headers, hidden links, and sender information. To begin, you need to download a suspicious email to your computer.

For Gmail Users

Open the email, click the three vertical dots (More) in the top right corner, and select "Download message".

For Outlook Users

Open the email, click "More actions" (...), and select "Download". Alternatively, you can double-click the email to open it in a new window, click File, and choose Save As.

2 Use the Standard Upload Scanner

If you want a comprehensive report on the entire email, use the main Upload tool located on the Home page.

  • Select your AI Model: Choose between available models (e.g., faster scanning vs. deeper analysis).
  • Upload: Click the upload area and select the .eml file you downloaded in Step 1.
  • Analyze: Click "Analyze Email" to generate a full report detailing malicious links, spoofed domains, and a general threat assessment.
Go to Upload Scanner

3 Explore the Real-Time Scan Tool

The Real-Time Scan tool allows you to interactively investigate specific parts of an email text without waiting for a full report.

  • Extract Content: Upload your .eml file to strip away formatting and view the raw text.
  • Click Highlights: PhishGuard automatically highlights suspicious elements like urgent keywords, emails, and URLs. Click them for an instant AI breakdown.
  • Custom Selection: Highlight any custom block of text with your mouse. A "🔍 Analyze Selection" tooltip will appear. Click it to send that specific text to the AI.
  • Threat Scores: Instantly see if a highlighted phrase registers as a High (Red), Medium (Yellow), or Low (Green) threat.
Try Real-Time Scan

4 Review and Act

Once PhishGuard has provided its analysis, review the AI Insights carefully. If an email is flagged with a high threat score, do not click any links or download any attachments within the original email. Mark the email as Spam/Phishing in your email client and delete it.
