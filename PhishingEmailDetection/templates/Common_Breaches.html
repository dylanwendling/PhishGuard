🚨 Common Breaches

Understand the most frequent tactics cybercriminals use to compromise accounts.

Email Phishing

Attackers send fake emails that appear to come from trusted organizations such as banks, schools, or online services. These emails often contain suspicious links, urgent messages, or fake login requests intended to steal user credentials.

Spear Phishing

This is a targeted form of phishing aimed at a specific person or group. The attacker uses personal details to make the message appear more believable and increase the chances of the victim clicking a malicious link or sharing confidential information.

Malicious Attachments

Some phishing messages include infected attachments disguised as invoices, forms, or urgent documents. Opening these files can install malware or redirect the user to harmful websites.

Business Email Compromise (BEC)

In BEC attacks, cybercriminals impersonate high-level executives or trusted partners to trick employees into transferring money or sharing sensitive data. These attacks often involve social engineering and careful research on the target organization.
